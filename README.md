# My attempt at making a 40% keyboard pcb
#### after making the usb hub on the hackclub tutorial i decided to challenge my self to make a 40% keyboard pcb and schematic. As this was my first time, i watched a couple of youtube videos and read a few guides on how to do it before i began. initially i struggled to find the right components but after some luck i managed to find the right mx switches which i already have and the 12 pin and 4 pin molexes. the layout went ok until i realised that i counted it wrong but i did it


#### the schematic consists of a large keyboard structure with 12 keys in a row adn 4 rows i remembered to leave spaces for the enter key and the space bar on the model. then i named each individual key and wired them up. i wired the space bar up last before then putting it in the pcb! i didnt sadly manage to have time to do the pcb due to some... personal issues and then decided to leave the project at the schematic point. the pcb however looked okay. i spent most of my time researching and looking at designs for me to follow along with and it was a cool experience. <img width="511" height="487" alt="Screenshot 2026-06-01 at 12 29 19" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6f5f8036-f7f8-4b9c-a95a-a5ea38c52570" />

### The bill of materials (BOM) is below  
It consists of the following:

- **Cherry MX1A‑11NW Switches** — 42 units  
  - Designators: A, APPLE, B, BACKSPACE, C, CAPS, COMMA, COMMAND, CONTROL, D, DOT, DOWN, E, F, G, H, I, J, K, L, LEFT, M, N, O, OPTION, P, Q, R, RETURN, RIGHT, S, SHIFT, SPACE, T, U, UP, V, W, X, Y, Z  
  - Footprint: SW‑TH_MX1A‑11NW (CENTER_FIX)  
  - Manufacturer: CHERRY  

- **1N4148 Diodes** — 44 units  
  - Designators: D1–D44  
  - Footprint: DO‑35_BD2.2‑L4.0‑D0.50_1N4148  

- **Molex 5267 12‑Pin Connector** — 1 unit  
  - Designator: MOLEX_COL  
  - Footprint: MOLEX_5267_12P  

- **Molex 5267 4‑Pin Connector** — 1 unit  
  - Designator: MOLEX_ROW  
  - Footprint: MOLEX_5267_4P
